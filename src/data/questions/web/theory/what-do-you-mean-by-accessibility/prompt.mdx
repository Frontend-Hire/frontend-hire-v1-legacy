# What do you mean by Accessibility?

> Accessibility is a tough topic, in fact, there are dedicated roles called Accessibility Engineering who deal exclusively with web accessibility.

## Answer

Accessibility in general means "Can everyone access a product or tool." In web development too, it means designing and building websites and applications that can be used by everyone, including people with disabilities. It ensures that digital content and functionality are perceivable, operable, understandable, and robust for all users, regardless of their abilities or the devices they use.

## Follow Up Questions

### Can you name some common accessibility guidelines or standards?

The most widely recognized are the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG), developed by the W3C.

### What are some key elements to consider when making a website accessible?

Some Key Elements Include:

- Proper HTML structure and semantic markup
- Adequate color contrast
- Alternative text for images
- Keyboard navigation support
- Descriptive link text
- Proper form labeling
- Scalable text and responsive design

### How do you test for web accessibility?

- Automated tools like WAVE or aXe
- Manual testing with keyboard navigation
- Screen reader testing (e.g., NVDA, JAWS)
- Color contrast checkers
- User testing with individuals who have disabilities

### Why is accessibility important from a business perspective?

- Expands the potential user base
- Improves SEO and overall user experience
- Reduces legal risks and potential lawsuits
- Demonstrates corporate social responsibility
